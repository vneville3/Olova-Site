Stories and background
Vaughan Smith-Neville

The work makes more sense when you know where it comes from.

OLOVA is not a studio invented out of thin air. It comes from decades of skateboarding, advocacy, photography, travel, community work, and a long habit of looking at public space like it could be better than what got built last time.

Background

Skate roots, advocacy, and the longer line behind the work.

The point is simple: show where the work actually comes from.
01

Skate roots

Skateboarding is not decoration on top of the work. It is the base layer. The eye for flow, edge detail, social space, and what actually gets used came from years of skating real spots and paying attention.

02

Advocacy roots

The project interest is not just visual. It comes from real involvement around youth space, local effort, coalition work, and the stubborn reality that good public space usually happens because somebody kept pushing for it.

03

Visual roots

Photography, rendering, and presentation work all feed the same thing: helping people see a project clearly enough to care, discuss it properly, and make stronger decisions before the window closes.
