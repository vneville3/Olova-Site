Community-linked work
Belong, advocacy, and local support

Some projects matter even when they are not the main revenue lane.

The community side of OLOVA exists to make room for the kinds of projects that build local culture, support youth space, and deserve real backing. It is not charity branding. It belongs here because the work is real, the history is real, and the local connections matter.

Featured project

Belong Centre is one of the clearest examples of this lane.

A community project Vaughan helped support through concept input, design visualization, and hands-on ramp-building work. The role is real, but the claims stay measured. The point is not to inflate the contribution. It is to show how design thinking, practical effort, and local advocacy can all meet in the same project.
