Belong Youth Centre

Belong is what happens when a dead building gets a second life.

Set inside the former Rocky Point Taphouse in Port Moody, Belong is an indoor skatepark and youth-focused gathering space built to give young people somewhere real to go. Not just to pass through, but to skate, make, meet up, put on events, and be part of something local.

The project came together through local effort, volunteer labour, artists, and youth involvement. That is why it feels lived in instead of packaged.

Why it matters

Indoor youth space like this is rare.

Belong fills a gap that ordinary drop-ins and rec schedules do not. It is not just a lounge, and it is not just a park. It is one of those rare indoor spaces where skating, art, music, events, and hanging out can all live under the same roof without feeling forced.

That matters in the Tri-Cities, where there are not enough low-barrier places for young people to gather once the weather turns or the city gets too expensive and too managed.

Built space Second life

A reused building with enough character left in it to still feel human.

That is part of why Belong works. It does not feel scrubbed into a blank room. The bones are still there, and the project is stronger for it.

Not polished into nothing

Why OLOVA backs it

Because projects like this do not happen by accident.

Good youth projects do not usually fail because nobody cares. They fail because the momentum slips, the space gets misread, or the idea never gets clear enough for other people to rally around. Belong is a strong local example of what it looks like when a project gets enough vision, enough work, and enough people around it to actually move.

OLOVA’s role here comes from real involvement, concept input, visual development, and practical support around a project trying to do something useful for Port Moody youth culture.

What made it move
  • A real building with street presence instead of another hidden room
