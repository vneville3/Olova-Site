Community-linked work
Belong, advocacy, and local support

Backing the right local projects is part of the work.

Community work sits inside OLOVA because some projects deserve real support, not just polite approval from a distance. That includes youth space, local advocacy, and practical contributions to efforts that strengthen skate culture in the Tri-Cities.

Belong Centre is one of the clearest examples of this work.

A community project Vaughan helped support through concept input, design visualization, and hands-on ramp-building work. The contribution is real, the claims stay measured, and the project shows how design thinking, practical effort, and local advocacy can meet in the same place.
